    Gran Cenote - Quintana Roo, Mexico


    Gran Cenote is a very impressive tourist attractions. It is the most popular scuba diving locating in the Yucatan Penninsula, and has a 4.5☆ rating on Trip Advisor. Gran Cenote is what some would call an underwater cave. Once equipped with the proper gear, you dive down into the depths. You re-emerge inside the cave. You can take advantage of the beautiful diamonds in the cave. Fun fact: If you flick diamonds inside of the cave, different diamonds make different sounds, almost like a piano. To visit and learn about Gran Cenote, visit their website here.


    Popocatépetl - Mexico Puebla Morelos, Mexico


    Popocatépetl(PTL) is an active volano in Morelos, Mexico. PTL is very special because there are not many active volcanoes in the world today. Surprisingly, PTL is directly next to a town, a direct hazard for the population. If you wish to visit PTL, then make sure you are expected for all outcomes. Here are some quick stats: Elevation: 17,802 ft, First Hiked in: 1519, Last Eruption: 2004-2019, Amount of Eruptions since discovered: 20. If you feel scared, don't be. Every individual who has hiked up PTL has said it was an adventure of their lifetime. Learn more a Popocatépetl here

    Chichén Itzá - Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico


    Chichén Itzá(CI) is an incredible sight. So incredible, that in 2007 it was elected as one of the new 7 wonders of the world. In Mexico, it is the 2nd most visited archaeological site. CI existed during the reign of the mayans, and through educated guesses we can assume that this was used as a place of religion/worship. One of the coolest parts about CI is it's age. CI has been standing for over 100 years! For fun I calculated it in seconds, and it's around 3,155,695,200 of them! To visit and learn about CI, go here.
